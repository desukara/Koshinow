Why Foxjin Exists

This was never just about filling a classroom.

James Simmons did not come to Japan planning to become an English teacher. He came to build a life with his wife. Before Japan, he had spent years developing as an instructor in the United States Army, where teaching meant responsibility, preparation, leadership, and trust. Later, his academic experience at Harvard University helped sharpen the way he thinks: carefully, critically, and with attention to the systems behind people's problems.

But like many native English speakers who build a life in Japan, James quickly learned that professional options for foreigners can be narrow. English teaching became the path available. At first, he was honest about the challenge. Military instruction had prepared him to train adults under pressure, and growing up around a large family had made him comfortable with people and energy, but standing in front of a classroom of students required a new kind of humility.

So he learned. He adapted. He listened. He worked across different schools, different student ages, different classroom cultures, and different expectations. Over time, one thing became clear: his gift was not only teaching English. His gift was connecting with people on their level and helping them feel confident enough to learn.

Then another pattern became impossible to ignore. Schools would bring him in as a teacher, but what they often truly needed was temporary, reliable, professional support while they solved a bigger staffing problem. A teacher had left. A schedule had changed. A gap had appeared. The school did not always need to rush into a permanent hire. It needed breathing room.

That realization became Foxjin. Not a recruitment agency. Not a faceless dispatch company. Foxjin is founder-led teaching support designed to protect students, support school owners, and give good schools the time they need to make good decisions.

The Problem Foxjin Solves

When a staffing gap appears, pressure moves fast.

Every school owner knows the feeling. A teacher resigns. A visa issue appears. A class suddenly needs coverage. Enrollment grows faster than expected. A schedule that looked stable on Monday can become a serious problem by Friday.

But students still arrive. Parents still expect consistency. Staff still need direction. And the school still has to protect the trust it has spent years building.

In those moments, the worst decision is often the rushed one. Foxjin gives schools a different option: experienced teaching support that keeps classes moving while the school takes the time to choose carefully.

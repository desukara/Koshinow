Teaching support when your school needs coverage.

When a teacher is absent, resigns suddenly, or a hiring process takes longer than expected, schools need reliable lesson coverage. Below are answers to common questions about temporary teaching support from Foxjin / 狐ジン.

Foxjin Answers

Open the question you need.

Some schools contact Foxjin during an urgent staffing problem. Others reach out before anything goes wrong. Either way, the goal is the same: keep the classroom steady while the school finds the right path forward.

Can you provide substitute teaching?

Yes. Foxjin / 狐ジン may be available to help schools maintain lesson continuity when a teacher is absent, unavailable, ill, resigns suddenly, or when unexpected schedule changes create a staffing gap.

Can you help during a hiring period?

Yes. Temporary teaching support may be available while your school interviews, evaluates, selects, and onboards a new long-term teacher.

Can you accept same-day requests?

Same-day requests may occasionally be possible, but cannot be guaranteed. Availability depends on existing teaching commitments, travel time, location, and class schedule.

Can you provide next-day support?

Next-day support is often easier to arrange than same-day coverage. Schools are encouraged to contact Foxjin / 狐ジン as early as possible when a staffing issue is expected.

Why should schools contact you in advance?

Many schools reach out before an emergency happens so they already have a teaching support contact available. Establishing contact early can make it easier to respond when urgent coverage is needed.

What types of schools do you support?

Support may be available for eikaiwa schools, children's English programs, adult conversation classes, business English training, and other English education settings.

What areas do you serve?

Support is primarily available throughout Tokyo. Depending on scheduling, travel time, and location, Chiba, Saitama, and Kanagawa may also be possible.

Can you teach from our existing curriculum?

Yes. Lessons can usually be taught using your school's existing textbooks, lesson plans, curriculum, worksheets, or classroom materials.

What age groups can you teach?

Lesson support may be available for children, teenagers, university students, adult learners, and business professionals, depending on the lesson style and school requirements.

Can inquiries be made in Japanese?

Yes. Japanese email inquiries are welcome. For telephone inquiries, please note that communication is conducted in English.

Can we contact you before we actually need support?

Absolutely. Schools often contact Foxjin / 狐ジン in advance so they have a reliable teaching support option available if a future absence, resignation, or staffing shortage occurs.

What information should we include when contacting you?

Please include your school location, requested date and time, student age group, lesson type, class length, materials available, and whether the request is urgent or for future planning.
